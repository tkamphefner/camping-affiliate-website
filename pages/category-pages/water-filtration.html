Clean Water Anywhere You Go

Access to clean, safe drinking water is essential for any camping or hiking trip. Whether you're backpacking in remote wilderness or car camping at a developed site, proper hydration and water purification equipment can make or break your outdoor experience.

What to Consider When Choosing Water Filtration

  • Filtration Method: Mechanical, chemical, UV, or combination
  • Filter Size: Measured in microns, smaller filters out more contaminants
  • Flow Rate: How quickly water passes through the filter
  • Capacity: How much water can be filtered before replacement
  • Weight: Important for backpacking and hiking
  • Ease of Use: Especially important in challenging conditions

Water Filtration & Hydration Buying Guide

Types of Water Treatment

  • Filters: Physical removal of bacteria and protozoa
  • Purifiers: Remove viruses in addition to bacteria and protozoa
  • Chemical Treatment: Tablets or drops that kill pathogens
  • UV Treatment: Uses ultraviolet light to neutralize microorganisms
  • Boiling: Traditional method that kills all pathogens

Understanding Micron Ratings

The smaller the micron rating, the more contaminants a filter can remove:

  • 1.0 - 0.2 microns: Removes bacteria and protozoa
  • 0.1 microns: Removes most bacteria and all protozoa
  • 0.02 microns and smaller: Can remove some viruses

For international travel or questionable water sources, look for purifiers that can remove viruses (which are typically smaller than 0.02 microns).

Hydration Systems

  • Water Bottles: Simple, reliable, available in various materials
  • Hydration Reservoirs: Bladders with drinking tubes for hands-free hydration
  • Collapsible Bottles: Space-saving when empty, good for backpacking
  • Filter Bottles: Combine storage and filtration in one unit
  • Insulated Bottles: Keep water cool in hot conditions

Considerations for Different Activities

  • Day Hiking: Water bottles or small reservoirs, lightweight filter
  • Backpacking: Lightweight, high-capacity systems, durable filters
  • International Travel: Purifiers that remove viruses, compact systems
  • Group Camping: High-volume gravity filters, multiple containers
  • Winter Camping: Insulated systems to prevent freezing
