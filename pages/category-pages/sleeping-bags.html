What to Consider When Choosing a Sleeping Bag

  • Temperature Rating: What weather conditions will you be camping in?
  • Weight: Will you be carrying it long distances?
  • Fill Type: Down or synthetic insulation?
  • Shape: Mummy, rectangular, or double?
  • Size: Will it accommodate your height and build comfortably?
  • Packability: How compact does it need to be?

Sleeping Bag Buying Guide

Temperature Ratings

  • Summer Season: 30°F and higher (0°C and higher)
  • 3-Season: 15°F to 30°F (-9°C to 0°C)
  • Winter: 15°F and lower (-9°C and lower)

Note: Always choose a sleeping bag rated for temperatures lower than you expect to encounter.

Insulation Types

  • Down: Excellent warmth-to-weight ratio, highly compressible, but loses insulating properties when wet
  • Synthetic: Maintains insulation when wet, dries quickly, less expensive, but heavier and bulkier
  • Hybrid: Combines down and synthetic materials for balanced performance

Sleeping Bag Shapes

  • Mummy: Tapered design for maximum warmth and minimum weight, snug fit
  • Rectangular: More spacious, allows more movement, but less thermally efficient
  • Semi-Rectangular/Barrel: Balance between warmth and space
  • Double: Designed for two people, typically rectangular
