What to Consider When Choosing Camping Furniture

  • Weight: How portable does it need to be?
  • Packed Size: How much space do you have for transport?
  • Durability: Will it withstand your intended use?
  • Comfort: How long will you be sitting?

Camping Furniture Buying Guide

Types of Camping Chairs

  • Traditional Camp Chairs: Folding chairs with four legs and armrests
  • Ultralight Chairs: Minimalist designs weighing under 2 pounds
  • Luxury Recliners: Feature reclining positions and extra padding
  • Rockers & Gliders: Provide rocking or swinging motion
  • Ground Chairs: Low-profile chairs that sit directly on the ground

Camping Tables

A good camping table makes meal prep, dining, and game nights much more enjoyable. Consider these factors:

  • Size & Weight: Match to your group size and transportation needs
  • Height: Standard (28-30"), low (23-27"), or adjustable
  • Material: Aluminum (lightweight), steel (durable), plastic (affordable)
  • Folding Mechanism: Roll-top, accordion, or bi-fold designs

Hammocks

  • Single vs. Double: Singles are lighter; doubles offer more space
  • Material: Nylon (lightweight, quick-drying) or cotton (comfortable, heavier)
  • Suspension System: Straps, carabiners, or rope systems
  • Weight Capacity: Typically 300-400 lbs for singles, 400-500 lbs for doubles
  • Accessories: Bug nets, rain tarps, and underquilts for extended use

Weight Considerations

  • Ultralight (under 2 lbs): Best for backpacking and hiking
  • Lightweight (2-5 lbs): Good balance for most camping trips
  • Standard (5-10 lbs): Offers more comfort for car camping
  • Heavy (10+ lbs): Maximum comfort but limited portability
